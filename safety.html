Regulatory Compliance

Every Lift International project meets or exceeds the safety requirements of all applicable regulatory bodies. Our compliance framework includes:

  • OSHA — Occupational Safety and Health Administration standards for crane operations
  • ANSI/ASME B30 — American National Standards for below-the-hook lifting devices and crane safety
  • ISO 9001:2015 — Quality management systems for consistent service delivery
  • Local Regulations — Full compliance with country-specific safety laws in every market we serve

Equipment Inspection Protocol

All equipment undergoes a rigorous multi-stage inspection process before, during, and after every deployment:

  • Pre-Deployment: Full mechanical and structural inspection, load testing, and safety system verification
  • Daily On-Site: Operator pre-shift checks including controls, wire ropes, hydraulics, and safety limiters
  • Monthly: Detailed maintenance reviews and component wear assessments
  • Third-Party Annual: Independent certified inspectors verify structural integrity and load capacity annually

Operator Certification & Training

Our operators are our most important safety asset. Every operator in our network meets strict qualification standards:

  • NCCCO / CPCS Certified — nationally or internationally recognized crane operator certification
  • Equipment-Specific Training — validated competency on the exact crane model being operated
  • Annual Refresher Courses — ongoing training on regulatory changes, new techniques, and incident lessons learned
  • Drug & Alcohol Testing — pre-employment and random testing programs for all field personnel

Lift Planning & Risk Assessment

Every lift begins with a thorough planning and risk assessment process to identify and mitigate hazards before work begins:

  • Engineered Lift Plans — detailed plans for critical lifts including load charts, rigging diagrams, and ground conditions
  • Job Hazard Analysis (JHA) — systematic hazard identification and control measures for every work scope
  • Pre-Lift Briefings — mandatory toolbox talks and crew briefings before any lifting operation
  • Weather Monitoring — real-time weather tracking with strict wind-speed limits for all crane types
